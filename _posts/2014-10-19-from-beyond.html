I heard her voice
and oh,
there rang beneath
well spoken lines
the quaking of a soul
alive and bound,

I did not know it then,
but yet,
she lived
beyond the grave
and said such things
that left me
mad, as mad as she.

She flittered through
the gates of hell,
with tulips
in her hand,
a ghastly veil
upon her face
that hid the sinful sleep
in which she lies.

She loved me well
and brief,
and called me darling
in a mockery of virtue
soured by fever.
Perhaps she thought she was
some species
of virgin come again,
my own lady lazarus
who stirs,
who does not sleep.
